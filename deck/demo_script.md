# TRINETRA — 3-minute demo video script

Target: **3:00**. Unlisted YouTube or public Google Drive. Screen-record the
deployed Slate URL at 1366×800+, dark room UI. Speak to the judges' rubric:
agentic AI · reduce investigation time · networks · explainable · on Catalyst.

Tips: rehearse once; keep the cursor deliberate; pre-load the app on the Network
view; have the queries copy-pasteable so typing is instant.

---

### 0:00–0:20 — Hook (problem)
**On screen:** title slide / console idle.
> "Karnataka Police records crime across 1,100 stations — but it lives in Excel
> silos. When an FIR comes in, finding linked cases takes investigators *days*.
> Meet TRINETRA — an AI agent that does it in seconds, in Kannada or English."

### 0:20–0:55 — Conversational + network (the core)
**Do:** type / speak *"Who connects these chain-snatching cases in Bengaluru South?"*
**On screen:** answer appears; network graph animates; Suresh M. node pulses red.
> "I ask in plain language. The agent turns it into a database query, and instead
> of a table it shows me *understanding* — four FIRs tied to one offender through
> a shared phone and a mule account. An organised ring, surfaced instantly."
**Do:** click Suresh M. → inspector.
> "Risk 87 — and it's explainable: network centrality, priors, MO consistency."

### 0:55–1:20 — Explainability (judges love this)
**Do:** expand **"view query & reasoning"** under the answer.
> "Every answer is auditable. Here's the exact ZCQL the agent generated, and its
> reasoning steps — with each FIR cited. Built for law-enforcement accountability."

### 1:20–1:45 — City map (spatiotemporal)
**Do:** click the **map** rail icon; drag the time slider to Evening.
> "Crime on a live map of Bengaluru. Chain-snatching clusters in the south,
> evenings 8 to 10 PM — so patrols go where crime *will* be, not where it was."

### 1:45–2:10 — Money trail + similar cases (leads)
**Do:** ask *"Trace the money trail"* → graph highlights the mule account.
> "It follows the money — a suspected mule account linking the cases."
**Do:** ask *"Find similar cases to FIR 2291"* → timeline of look-alikes.
> "And it suggests leads — other cases with the same modus operandi."

### 2:10–2:30 — Voice + Kannada + forecast
**Do:** toggle **ಕನ್ನಡ**, click mic, speak a query; then open **Alerts**.
> "Full Kannada voice for field officers. And it's proactive — a 7-day forecast
> flags BLR South up 180%, before the next crime."

### 2:30–2:50 — Built on Catalyst (architecture)
**On screen:** cut to deck architecture slide (or say over the app).
> "All on Zoho Catalyst — Slate frontend, Functions, Qwen via QuickML, Zia voice,
> Data Store. Role-based access, audit logs, and a SELECT-only query gate that
> blocks any unsafe query. Production-grade and secure."

### 2:50–3:00 — Close (impact)
**On screen:** network graph or title.
> "TRINETRA — the third eye that sees the hidden links between crimes, criminals
> and money. From days to seconds. Thank you."
